Life Story

The late William Kimanzi was born in 1957 at Ithiani Village in Kitui County. He was the sixthborn to the late Kimanzi Maingi and the late Syombua Kimanzi. He was a brother to Kasyoka Kimanzi, Mwikali Kimanzi, Kimoni Kimanzi, the late Malonza, the late Kilugya, the late Mwamba, the late Richard, the late Musyimi, and the late David. He was a brother-in-law to the late John Mweu, Andrew Kyambi, Muinde Katunga, Philip Katunga, and Muindi, among others. He was a nephew to the late Kalekye Maingi, the late John Ngungui, and Mary Ngungui, among others. He was also a son-in-law to the late Katunga and Beatrice Kaeke Katunga.

🎓

Education

William began his educational journey at Ithiani Primary School before proceeding to Mbusyani. The foundation he received through education played a significant role in shaping him into the responsible man we all came to know and admire.

💛

Marriage & Family

In due time, William entered into a loving union with his dear wife, Margaret Mwende Katunga. Their marriage was blessed with seven children: Jacinta Kanini, Fredrick Ngungui, Catherine Kaeke (Beatrice), Antony Kimanzi, Rosemary William, the late Syombua Mulela, and Mercy Kasyoka.

He was a devoted father who loved his children dearly and worked hard to provide for them and guide them through life. As his family grew, he warmly welcomed his sons-in-law and daughters-in-law—Joseph Kisabit, Joseph Kimani, Brian Mulavi, Eunice Maina, and Purity Ngami—treating them as his own children and valuing family unity.

William was further blessed with grandchildren: Beatrice Kibor, Joyce Wanjiru, Margaret Mwende, Joy Wangari, Blessing Kanini, Margaret Cherotich, Godwill Mumo, Kaylee Mwende, Emmanuel Baraka, and Princess Wambui, all of whom brought him immense joy and fulfillment.

💼

Work Life

Throughout his life, William was a hardworking man. He worked at the Coast Bus before, later joining Bata Shoe Company. Through dedication, perseverance, and a strong sense of responsibility, he was able to provide for his family and uphold the dignity of honest labor.

✝️

Christian Life

William was a man of faith who loved God and trusted in Him. He believed strongly in prayers and Christian values. He was a strong member of St. Kizito Small Christian Community of St. Francis of Asis Ithimani Station.

🕊️

Final Journey

In the final chapter of his life, William courageously battled a chest and lung infection that significantly affected his breathing and led to multiple hospital admissions. Despite these trials, he remained steadfast—facing each challenge with remarkable courage, hope, and resilience. His strength during this time was a source of inspiration to his family and all who stood by him. His demise has deeply saddened everyone around him.

🌿

A Final Farewell

On 27th March 2026, God, in His divine wisdom, called His faithful servant home to eternal rest. May God rest him in eternal peace.

William leaves behind his loving wife, his children, sons-in-law, daughters-in-law, and grandchildren. Though he is no longer with us in the physical sense, his legacy of love, strength, and faith will forever live on in the hearts of all who knew and cherished him.
